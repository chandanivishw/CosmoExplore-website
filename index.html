NASA’s SpaceX CRS-33

Rendezvous and docking coverage of NASA’s SpaceX CRS-33 commercial resupply services mission to the International Space Station

NASA’s SpaceX CRS-33

At 2:45 a.m. EDT on Sunday, Aug. 24, over 5,000 pounds of science investigations and cargo launched to the International Space Station on the SpaceX Dragon spacecraft for the company’s 33rd commercial resupply services mission for NASA. Mission coverage resumes at 6 a.m. EDT on Monday, Aug. 25.

Earth Science

Explore our changing planet

For more than 50 years, NASA satellites have provided data on Earth’s land, water, air, temperature, and climate. NASA’s Earth Information Center allows visitors to see how our planet is changing in six key areas: sea level rise and coastal impacts, health and air quality, wildfires, greenhouse gases, sustainable energy, and agriculture.
